Symatique Internship
PawTracker in Depth

This page documents the internship execution in professional depth: context, business problem, architecture decisions, implementation strategy, technical system, delivered artifacts, and impact observed by stakeholders.

Internship scope

Role: IoT + Full-Stack intern. Mission: ship a practical monitoring ecosystem from device telemetry to decision-ready interfaces for both pet owners and veterinarians.

Core challenge

Build a reliable, low-latency, and interpretable health intelligence workflow on constrained devices while keeping UX simple and actionable.

Background + Problem

What issue we had to solve

Business and user pain points

  • Health anomalies were identified too late in daily practice.
  • Existing tools were fragmented across GPS, notes, and reminders.
  • No centralized timeline connecting vitals with veterinary actions.
  • Owners needed clear guidance, not raw sensor numbers.

Operational constraints

  • Wearable battery and connectivity limitations in outdoor usage.
  • Noise and drift in low-cost sensor streams.
  • Need to support mobile and web user roles simultaneously.
  • Mandatory data consistency across app and vet portal.
Our Solution

Product and engineering approach

Design direction

We treated the system as a single product platform, not separate apps. Every design choice connected to one objective: convert sensor streams into trusted decisions quickly for both owners and clinicians.

Deliverable stack

  • Wearable firmware and telemetry protocol.
  • Backend ingestion, storage, and model inference services.
  • Android app with location and health alert workflows.
  • VetLink web panel for records and follow-up operations.
Technical System

How the system worked in practice

Device Layer

ESP32 sampled movement and vital indicators with periodic buffering and packet tagging by pet profile.

Transport Layer

MQTT topics streamed normalized payloads to cloud services with retry strategy for network instability.

Intelligence Layer

Regression + classification pipelines inferred risk states using breed and age-aware feature calibration.

Application Layer

Android + Angular surfaces converted model output into alerts, records, and appointment-ready context.

Execution Timeline

What we did step by step

Week 01

Requirement workshops and risk analysis with engineering and veterinary stakeholders.

Week 02

Sensor data schema and messaging contracts for firmware to backend compatibility.

Week 03

MQTT ingestion services, persistence layer, and health feature extraction pipeline.

Week 04

First anomaly scoring logic and threshold tuning on pilot telemetry sessions.

Week 05

Android UX integration: map, health cards, push notifications, and state handling.

Week 06

VetLink integration, quality hardening, final validation, and deployment handoff.
