Background

Problem Identified

Why this mattered

Pet owners and veterinarians usually react after visible symptoms. Existing consumer trackers focused on GPS, but lacked multi-signal health interpretation and context-aware alerting. The challenge was to move from passive tracking to preventive monitoring.

Constraints and risks

  • Low-power wearable hardware with noisy sensor streams.
  • Unstable mobile connectivity and roaming outdoor conditions.
  • Need for interpretable scores, not black-box outputs.
  • Cross-role workflows: owner app + veterinarian dashboard.
Our Solution

End-to-End Monitoring System

Product strategy

We designed a closed loop: wearable acquisition, low-latency ingestion, model scoring, and role-specific interfaces. Owners receive clear alerts and location context; veterinarians get longitudinal trend visibility and patient records.

Real-time pipelineRisk scoringAlert engineRole-based UX

What we delivered

  • ESP32 wearable firmware for activity and vitals capture.
  • MQTT event streaming with backend processing services.
  • Android app with live GPS, health cards, and notifications.
  • VetLink Angular web portal with records and follow-up.
Technical System

How we solved it technically

Data Ingestion

Wearable telemetry is published over MQTT topics segmented by pet profile and stream type. Backend consumers validate, normalize, and queue packets for feature extraction.

ESP32MQTTSpring Boot

ML Inference Layer

Supervised regression and classification pipelines convert temporal features into risk states calibrated by breed and age. Threshold logic was tuned to reduce false alarms.

Scikit-learnFeature WindowsCalibration

Experience Layer

The mobile app prioritizes rapid signal comprehension. VetLink extends this with case history and appointment context to support operational clinical decisions.
